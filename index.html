US Compliance Information

Last Updated: March 12, 2025

US Regulatory Compliance Overview

American Marketing Suite is committed to helping our customers comply with all applicable US regulations. This document provides an overview of how our tools can be used in compliance with key US laws.

CAN-SPAM Act Compliance

The CAN-SPAM Act establishes requirements for commercial messages and gives recipients the right to opt-out. Our tools help you comply by:

  • Providing clear unsubscribe mechanisms that can be easily implemented in messages
  • Allowing you to maintain and honor opt-out lists
  • Enabling accurate "From," "To," and routing information in your messages
  • Supporting the inclusion of your physical postal address

TCPA Compliance

The Telephone Consumer Protection Act (TCPA) regulates telemarketing calls, SMS messages, and faxes. Our WhatsApp Sender helps you maintain compliance by:

  • Facilitating proper consent management before sending messages
  • Providing opt-out tracking and management
  • Limiting messaging hours to comply with TCPA time restrictions
  • Maintaining records of consent for each recipient

California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA)

For businesses that fall under CCPA regulations, our tools provide:

  • Data management features to help track what personal information is collected
  • Tools to facilitate consumer requests to access or delete their data
  • Options to implement and honor opt-out preferences
  • Templates for privacy notices and disclosures

FTC Endorsement Guidelines

When using our social media tools for product marketing:

  • We provide templates for clear disclosure of material connections
  • Our content library includes examples of compliant endorsement language
  • We offer guidance on proper disclosure placement

State-Specific Regulations

In addition to federal laws, we provide resources to help you navigate state-specific regulations, including:

  • California's CCPA and CPRA
  • Virginia's Consumer Data Protection Act
  • Colorado's Privacy Act
  • New York's SHIELD Act

Disclaimer

While we provide tools and resources to help you maintain compliance, we strongly recommend consulting with a qualified legal professional to ensure your specific marketing activities meet all applicable legal requirements. Regulatory compliance is ultimately the responsibility of your business.

GDPR Compliance

Last Updated: March 2025

Introduction to GDPR

The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) is a comprehensive privacy and data protection law that took effect on May 25, 2018, in the European Union (EU) and European Economic Area (EEA). At Digital Marketing Suite, we are committed to ensuring our products and practices comply with GDPR requirements, respecting and protecting the privacy rights of all our users.

This document outlines our approach to GDPR compliance and how we handle personal data in accordance with these regulations.

Our Role Under GDPR

Depending on how our tools are used, Digital Marketing Suite operates in the following capacities:

  • Data Controller: When we collect and process data about you as our customer or website visitor.
  • Data Processor: When you use our tools to process your customers' data.

As both a controller and processor, we have implemented comprehensive measures to fulfill our GDPR obligations and protect your rights and the rights of your customers.

Legal Basis for Processing

Under GDPR, we must have a valid legal basis for collecting and processing personal data. We rely on the following legal bases:

1. Contractual Necessity

We process data necessary to fulfill our contractual obligations to you when you purchase and use our tools. This includes:

  • Account setup and maintenance
  • Providing our services
  • Customer support
  • Processing payments

2. Legitimate Interests

We process data when we have a legitimate business interest that does not override your privacy rights, such as:

  • Improving and optimizing our services
  • Ensuring security and preventing fraud
  • Marketing our services to existing customers
  • Analytics to understand how our tools are used

3. Consent

Where required by law, we obtain your explicit consent before processing personal data, particularly for:

  • Marketing communications to prospective customers
  • Collecting certain types of cookies and tracking technologies
  • Processing special categories of personal data

You have the right to withdraw your consent at any time, which will not affect the lawfulness of processing based on consent before its withdrawal.

Data Subject Rights

The GDPR provides individuals (data subjects) with several rights regarding their personal data. We respect these rights and have implemented processes to facilitate their exercise.

Right to Access

You can request a copy of the personal data we hold about you, along with information about how we use it.

Right to Rectification

You can request that we correct any inaccurate or incomplete personal data we hold about you.

Right to Erasure (Right to be Forgotten)

You can request that we delete your personal data in certain circumstances, such as when the data is no longer necessary for the purposes for which it was collected.

Right to Restriction of Processing

You can request that we restrict the processing of your personal data in certain circumstances, such as when you contest the accuracy of the data.

Right to Data Portability

You can request to receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format, and to transmit this data to another controller.

Right to Object

You can object to the processing of your personal data in certain circumstances, particularly for direct marketing purposes or when processing is based on legitimate interests.

Rights Related to Automated Decision-Making

You have the right not to be subject to a decision based solely on automated processing that produces legal or similarly significant effects.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact our Data Protection Officer at privacy@digitalmarketingsuite.com.

Data Protection Measures

We have implemented appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk, including:

  • Encryption: We encrypt data in transit and at rest using industry-standard protocols.
  • Access Controls: We implement strict access controls and authentication mechanisms to ensure only authorized personnel can access personal data.
  • Regular Security Assessments: We conduct regular security assessments, vulnerability scans, and penetration tests.
  • Staff Training: We provide regular data protection and security training to our staff.
  • Data Minimization: We collect and retain only the data necessary for our specified purposes.
  • Backup and Recovery: We maintain regular backups and have established procedures for data recovery.

Data Protection Impact Assessments

We conduct Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs) when implementing new technologies or processing activities that are likely to result in a high risk to individuals' rights and freedoms.

Data Breach Notification

In the event of a personal data breach, we will notify the relevant supervisory authority without undue delay and, where feasible, within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach, unless the breach is unlikely to result in a risk to the rights and freedoms of individuals.

If the breach is likely to result in a high risk to your rights and freedoms, we will also notify you directly, where feasible.

International Data Transfers

When we transfer personal data outside the EU/EEA, we ensure that appropriate safeguards are in place, such as:

  • Adequacy decisions by the European Commission
  • Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)
  • Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs)
  • Other appropriate safeguards as required by the GDPR
